Interactive Figure 1: Traffic Composition

Toggle between absolute volume (EB/month) and percentage share using the buttons above the chart.

Viz 1: Traffic Composition

Video streaming has been the dominant category of global mobile traffic throughout 2018–2025, growing from 13.4 to 108.4 EB/month and raising its share from 59.9% to 75.9% of total traffic. Social Networking is the fastest-growing non-video category at 23.2% CAGR, while Web Browsing grew slowest at 11.4%, consistent with the shift from web-page based apps to mobile apps. Toggle between absolute volume and percentage share to observe how compositional dominance and growth rates differ across categories.

Interactive Figure 2: Regional Consumption Trends

Use the dropdown to isolate individual regions. All regions are shown by default.

Viz 2: Regional Consumption Trends

India & South Asia leads all regions in per-subscription consumption by 2025 at 36.0 GB/month, driven by aggressive low-cost unlimited data plans that made India one of the world's highest per-subscriber traffic markets despite its lower income level. Sub-Saharan Africa remains the lowest at 5.3 GB/month. The absolute gap between the highest and lowest consuming regions raised from 7.3 GB/month in 2018 to 30.7 GB/month in 2025, showing a digital equity tension, as percentage growth in lagging regions is not sufficient to close absolute gaps when leading regions are also growing rapidly. Use the dropdown to isolate individual regions.

Interactive Figure 3: Total Traffic and YoY Growth Rate

Bars show quarterly total traffic volume (left axis). The red line shows year-over-year growth rate (right axis).

Viz 3: Total Traffic and YoY Growth Rate

Global mobile data traffic grew from roughly 16 EB/month in early 2018 to more than 200 EB/month by late 2025. Year-over-year growth rates were highest in 2018–2019, before declining towards a more stable 20–25% range by 2024–2025. The bars show that absolute volume additions per quarter have continued to rise even as percentage growth rates decreased.

Interactive Figure 4: Catch-Up Dynamics

Each point is one region. X-axis: 2018 baseline consumption. Y-axis: CAGR 2018–2025. Color encodes 2025 consumption level. The dashed OLS line tests whether lower-baseline regions grew faster.

Viz 4: Catch-Up Dynamics

The cross-sectional OLS confirms a catch-up effect, as the slope on 2018 baseline consumption is −1.198 (p = 0.026), indicating that a one GB/month higher baseline is associated with a 1.20 percentage point lower annual growth rate over 2018–2025. However, percentage catch-up in growth rates does not imply convergence in absolute consumption. The absolute gap between the highest and lowest consuming regions increased from 7.3 to 30.7 GB/month over the same period, even as lower-baseline regions grew faster in percentage terms.

Interactive Figure 5: Regional Yearly Increase Map

Use the year slider to move from 2019 to 2025. Marker size shows the year-over-year increase in GB/month for each Ericsson region, while color distinguishes regions on the world map.

Viz 5: Regional Yearly Increase Map

Each marker represents one of the nine Ericsson reporting regions, positioned at the region's geographic center. Marker size reflects the year-over-year increase in GB/month per subscription for that region. Use the year slider to see how regional growth patterns shifted over 2018–2025. India & South Asia markets show a solid and consistent increase in average data consumption per subscription, indicating a strong potential for continuous rollout in 5G service. While the Sub-Saharan Africa market presents a decline in net increase of data consumption, due to the low ROI of 5G rollout and market maturity.
