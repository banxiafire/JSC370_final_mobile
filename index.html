Research findings

RQ1 — Traffic composition

Video is structurally dominant and accelerating

Video grew from 13.4 to 108.4 EB/month (34.8% CAGR), and its share of total traffic rose from 59.9% to 75.9%. Social networking grew fastest among non-video categories at 23.2%/year. Web browsing was slowest at 11.4%.

8.1× absolute volume increase

RQ2 — Regional divergence

The absolute gap is widening, not closing

The gap between highest- and lowest-consuming regions widened from 7.3 to 30.7 GB/month. India & South Asia leads at 36.0 GB/month, driven by low-cost unlimited plans. Sub-Saharan Africa remains lowest at 5.3 GB/month.

Gap widened 4.2× in 7 years

RQ3 — 5G & consumption

5G coverage tracks consumption growth closely

Pearson r = 0.994 between global 5G coverage and world-average per-subscription consumption, 2018–2025. Each percentage point of coverage corresponds to ~0.249 GB/month. Year is a confounding factor.

r = 0.994, p < 0.0001

RQ4 — Catch-up dynamics

Percentage catch-up ≠ absolute convergence

Cross-sectional OLS confirms catch-up: slope = −1.198 (p = 0.026), meaning higher-baseline regions grew slower in terms of percentage. But absolute gaps widened. Panel OLS finds 25.3% within-region annual growth after controlling for fixed effects (R² = 0.967).

slope = −1.20, p = 0.026

Core finding

Catch-up in %
≠ convergence in GB

The central digital equity tension of mobile connectivity.

Lower-consumption regions do grow faster in percentage terms. As the cross-sectional slope of −1.20 (p = 0.026) confirms this statistically. But because leading regions also keep growing, the absolute gap widened from 7.3 to 30.7 GB/month between 2018 and 2025. A user in India & South Asia gains ~3.4 GB/month more capacity each year. A user in Sub-Saharan Africa gains ~0.6 GB/month. The ratio is diverging, far behind converging.

Methods

Data & modeling approach

All data come from Ericsson Mobility Report CSV exports. Four datasets: traffic by category (EB/month), GB per subscription by region, 5G population coverage, and quarterly total traffic with YoY growth.

RQ1–3 use descriptive analysis and correlation. RQ4 uses two OLS models: a panel regression with region fixed effects (n=72 region-year observations) and a cross-sectional catch-up test (n=9 regions).

Model A — Panel OLS

log(GB/month)ᵢₜ = αᵢ + β·yearₜ + εᵢₜ

Model B — Cross-sectional OLS

CAGRᵢ = α + β·GB₂₀₁₈,ᵢ + εᵢ
